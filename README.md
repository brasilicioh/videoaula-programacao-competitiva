## ğŸ§  What is Competitive Programming?

**Competitive programming** is a mental sport where participants compete to solve algorithmic, logical, and mathematical problems as efficiently as possible.

Competitors receive several problem statements and must submit code that produces correct outputs for a set of test inputs â€” all under strict time and performance limits.  
Submissions are automatically evaluated, and points are awarded based on correctness and speed. The winner is the one who solves the most problems in the shortest time.

The sport encourages creativity, efficiency, and problem-solving skills, and is widely practiced in schools, universities, and online platforms around the world.

## ğŸ† A Brief History

Competitive programming originated in the 1970s at **Texas A&M University**, where small problem-solving contests were organized among students.  
Over the following decades, it evolved into international competitions such as the **ICPC (International Collegiate Programming Contest)**, which has gathered teams from more than 80 countries since 2005.
